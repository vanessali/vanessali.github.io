Codepath: iOS bootcamp

During an intensive 8-week bootcamp at Codepath, I developed a mini app each week for the first month, and a final project in the second month. We applied concepts learned during lectures such as networking, authentication, caching, MVC principles, UI/auto layout, animations, etc. to build Twitter, Yelp, and Rotten Tomatoes clients.

The final group project was an app designed specifically for a non-profit charter school, Kipp, to help teachers track student progress. One of the main principles distinguishing Kipp's program from other school systems is their focus on character development. A problem teachers and administrators have are the disparate systems they use to track progress not only across subjects, but also for character tracking and attendance. Our goal was to design and develop an app to allow teachers to take attendance, track character progress, and make note of which students to call out to celebrate achievements, or motivate for those falling behind by notifying parents or giving special attention in class.

Tools: Swift, XCode, Parse
